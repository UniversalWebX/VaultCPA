Question 01
What is estate planning and why does it matter?

Estate planning ensures your assets are distributed according to your wishes with minimal tax and delay. Without a plan, assets may face probate, unintended tax exposure, or family disputes. We build structures that make the transition clear and efficient.

Question 02
What is an estate freeze and who should consider it?

An estate freeze locks in the current value of your assets for tax purposes, shifting future growth to the next generation. It caps your capital gains exposure. The right timing depends on your business value, family situation, and succession goals.

Question 03
What taxes are triggered when someone passes away in Canada?

Canada has no inheritance tax, but a deemed disposition on death treats all assets as sold at fair market value, triggering capital gains. RRSPs and RRIFs are fully included in income unless transferred to a spouse. We plan to minimize these exposures in advance.

Question 04
Should I set up a family trust?

A family trust distributes income among family members, protects assets from creditors, and facilitates intergenerational wealth transfer. Whether it makes sense depends on your income level, assets, and family structure. We assess the tradeoffs before recommending one.

Question 05
How do I pass my business to the next generation tax-efficiently?

Options include a gradual share transfer, an estate freeze with a family trust, or a structured buy-out. The Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption may shelter a portion of the sale. The right approach depends on whether the successor is a family member, partner, or third party.

Question 06
What is probate and how can it be minimized?

Probate validates a will and authorizes the executor to distribute assets. In BC, probate fees are calculated on gross estate value. Strategies to minimize probate include holding assets jointly, designating beneficiaries on registered accounts, and using trusts.

Question 07
Do you work with estate lawyers?

Yes. We work alongside estate lawyers regularly. We focus on the tax and financial structuring layer — valuations, reorganizations, trust filings, and CRA compliance — while coordinating with your lawyer so the legal documentation reflects the plan accurately.

Question 08
When should I start estate planning?

As soon as you have assets worth protecting or people who depend on you. For business owners, the sooner the better — many strategies require years to implement. Waiting until illness or urgency limits your options. A free consultation is the right starting point.
