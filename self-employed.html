Question 01
Do I need to file a T1 if I'm self-employed?

Yes. All self-employed individuals must file a T1 return and report net business income. The filing deadline is June 15, but any balance owing is due by April 30. Filing late results in penalties even if no tax is owed.

Question 02
Do I need to register for GST/HST?

You must register once worldwide taxable revenues exceed $30,000 in a calendar quarter or over four consecutive quarters. You can also register voluntarily before that threshold to claim input tax credits. We handle registration and ongoing filing once enrolled.

Question 03
What expenses can I deduct as a contractor?

Eligible deductions include home office, vehicle, equipment, software, professional fees, advertising, and subcontractor costs. Expenses must be incurred to earn business income and reasonable in amount. We review your full profile to ensure every eligible deduction is captured.

Question 04
Do I have to pay CPP as a self-employed person?

Yes. Self-employed individuals pay both the employee and employer portions of CPP on net business income at a combined rate of 11.9%. We factor this into your tax planning and instalment calculations so there are no surprises at filing time.

Question 05
What are tax instalments and do I need to pay them?

If you owed more than $3,000 in net tax in the current or either of the two prior years, CRA requires quarterly instalments in March, June, September, and December. Missing instalments triggers interest. We calculate the right amount and remind you of each due date.

Question 06
Should I incorporate my contracting business?

Incorporation can reduce your tax rate, provide liability protection, and create income-splitting opportunities, but adds compliance costs and complexity. The right answer depends on your income level, personal needs, and growth plans. We walk you through the tradeoffs before you decide.

Question 07
Do I need to issue T4As to subcontractors?

Yes. If you pay a subcontractor more than $500 in a calendar year, you must issue a T4A slip by the last day of February. Failure to file T4As on time results in CRA penalties. We handle preparation and filing so you stay compliant.

Question 08
How much does it cost to have my self-employment taxes prepared?

Our fees depend on the complexity of your income, number of expense categories, and whether GST/HST filing is included. We provide a clear fixed-fee quote before any work begins. Book a free consultation and we will give you an accurate estimate.
